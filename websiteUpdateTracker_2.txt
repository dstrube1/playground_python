<h1>COVID Vaccine Scheduling</h1>

<p>Vaccine is in high demand and limited in quantity. Vaccine availability is the limiting factor for making appointments in the short term while vaccine providers across the state (i.e. health departments, hospitals, physicians offices, pharmacies, etc.) await their order allocations.</p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000;"><strong>Appointments are required and available for scheduling online only at this time. <span style="color: #ff0000;">Walk-ins cannot be </span></strong><span style="caret-color: #000000;"><b>accommodated. Appointments cannot be made by visiting or call our health centers.</b></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000080;"><strong>The Health Department is releasing appointments only for the amount of vaccine currently in stock. If there are no appointments available, that means we have scheduled all appointments to exhaust our supply of vaccine. We will not release additional appointments until we have received more doses of vaccine to ensure we can meet demand. Thank you for your patience.</strong> </span></p>

<h2><strong>What to wear</strong></h2>
<p>Vaccinations are given in the upper arm. Wear loose, comfortable clothing that will allow access to your upper arm while maintaining a reasonable amount of modesty.</p>
<h2><strong>What to expect</strong></h2>
<p>Masks are required throughout the vaccination site.</p>
<ul>
<li>When you arrive to the vaccination site, we request that you remain in your vehicle until 10 minutes before your appointment time.</li>
<li>When you approach the vaccination site entrance, an individual will confirm your appointment before allowing you inside. Individuals without appointments will not receive vaccine.</li>
<li>Please have your insurance card and identification ready, if applicable, at check-in. There is no charge for vaccine. Insurance will be filed, if available. This will not result in a charge to you.</li>
<li>Your second dose appointment will made at check-in.</li>
<li>Prior to receiving your vaccine, please let someone know if you need to use the restroom. After receiving the vaccine, you will not be able to leave the observation area for any reason for 15-30 minutes.</li>
<li>When your observation period is complete, visit the check-out area before you leave.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Second-dose appointments</h2>
<p>Second-dose appointments are made during the first-appointment visit.</p>
<p><strong>Moderna and Pfizer vaccines require individuals to return for a second vaccine after a few weeks of receiving the first dose to increase the vaccines&#8217; effectiveness.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Individuals do not need to have the second dose exactly at the interval recommended by the vaccine manufacturer. Vaccine experts have said there is no reason to think the second dose won&#8217;t be as effective if it is not received in a narrow window of time. Therefore, if the second dose of the vaccine is administered &gt;3 weeks after the first Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine dose or &gt;1 month after the first Moderna vaccine dose, there is no need to restart the series. </strong></p>
